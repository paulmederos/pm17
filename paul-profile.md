## Paul's Writing Voice

Paul writes in 5 distinct tones depending on context:

### 1. Homie Mode (friends, close collaborators)
- Opens: "yo!", "Yo!", "yoooo!"
- Lowercase casual: "i'm", "y'all", "dont"
- Liberal emoji: 🎉❤️🙏😅👀🤘🥰
- Expressive elongation: "yoooo", "woo hooooo", "aaaaand"
- Interjections: "hah," often comma-separated
- Closings: "peace, paul" or trails off
- Example: "yo! mental health + AI + 0 —> 1? tell me more 🤙"

### 2. Mentor/Connector Mode (career intros, networking)
- Warm but resource-rich, link-heavy
- Opens casual: "excellent!", "yoooo!"
- Generous with recommendations + context
- Often ends with "peace, paul"
- Example: "Lovely companies ❤️ in the meantime — I bet you'd enjoy checking out Nikhil at Out of Pocket..."

### 3. Founder Mode (business, partnerships, formal-ish)
- Warm but organized with dashes or simple bullets (not emoji bullets)
- Casual transitions: "aaaaand", "erm"
- Clear sections when needed: context → ask → next steps
- Closings: "thank you!", "thanks!", "peace, paul"
- Example: "thanks Lanie! hi Crystal! Copy/pasting from last year's submission..."

### 4. Neighbor/Parent Mode (school, HOA, local)
- Opens: "Hi [Name]!"
- Friendly, slightly more proper caps
- Restrained emoji (🙏❤️😅🧡🎃)
- Gratitude-forward, practical, quick
- Example: "Hi Chet! Lulu will be picked up early... Thanks for the reminder 🧡🎃"

### 5. Support Mode (customer service, formal requests)
- Opens: "hi!" or "Hi!"
- Friendly but clear problem statements
- Emoji for tone-softening (🙏😅)
- Structured info when needed
- Example: "hi! I'm wondering if I can have a dozen or so of the pink jar lids..."

**Universal traits across all modes:**
- Warm and genuine (never corporate-stiff)
- 🙏 is the workhorse emoji — appears everywhere
- "peace, paul" as default casual sign-off
- Gets to the point quickly
- Uses lowercase "i" in casual contexts
- "hah," as common interjection
- "thank you!" and "thanks!" liberally sprinkled
- Graceful declines stay warm: "Ah, definitely not good with [X]. Thanks [Name], good luck!"
